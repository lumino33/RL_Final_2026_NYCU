Feedback-Guided Reinforcement Learning for Diffusion-Based Autonomous Driving

Khanh-Phong Bui, Dinh-Hung Le, Yin-Yu Hsu
National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University
Reinforcement Learning 2026
Poster Code

Project summary: Fail in simulation. Reflect on the cause. Succeed in reality.

Abstract

End-to-end autonomous driving has recently benefited from Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and diffusion-based trajectory planners, enabling stronger reasoning and more reliable decision making. ReCogDrive combines these components with reinforcement learning and achieves state-of-the-art performance on autonomous driving benchmarks. However, the framework still suffers from two limitations: the lack of failure-aware learning and limited exploration during policy optimization. To address these issues, we introduce a failure-aware feedback mechanism that provides semantic explanations for unsuccessful behaviors and an ambiguity-driven trajectory selection strategy that promotes more informative exploration. Experimental results show that the proposed improvements consistently enhance driving performance and robustness, demonstrating the effectiveness of combining explicit failure feedback with exploration-oriented reinforcement learning for diffusion-based autonomous driving systems.

Baseline: ReCogDrive

Overview of ReCogDrive. ReCogDrive is an end-to-end autonomous driving framework that integrates Vision-Language Models with diffusion-based trajectory planning. The model first performs high-level reasoning about the driving scene and generates cognitive tokens that capture perception, prediction, and planning information. These tokens are then used to guide a diffusion planner, enabling the generation of safe, smooth, and physically feasible driving trajectories.

Training Pipeline and Model Architecture. ReCogDrive combines a Vision-Language Model (VLM) with a diffusion-based trajectory planner, where cognitive tokens generated by the VLM guide the denoising process. The framework is trained in three stages: Driving Pre-training for cognitive understanding, Imitation Learning for trajectory generation, and Reinforcement Learning for policy optimization. A Lightning Diffusion Transformer then integrates cognitive tokens, ego states, and historical actions to generate safe and feasible future trajectories.

Limitation 1: Lack of Failure-aware Learning. ReCogDrive relies solely on scalar rewards from the simulator to optimize its policy. While the model can identify unsuccessful trajectories, it lacks explicit information about the underlying causes of failure, such as collisions, lane deviations, or unsafe maneuvers. As a result, policy updates become less informative, limiting the model's ability to learn robust behaviors in challenging scenarios.

Limitation 2: Limited Exploration in RL Optimization. ReCogDrive relies on DiffGRPO to optimize trajectories, but the diffusion planner often generates highly similar trajectories within the same scenario. As a result, the reward variance becomes very small, leading to weak advantage signals and ineffective policy updates. This limited exploration prevents the policy from discovering better driving behaviors and reduces the effectiveness of reinforcement learning.

Proposed Method

To address the limitations of ReCogDrive, we introduce two complementary improvements. The first focuses on failure-aware learning by leveraging teacher feedback to provide semantic explanations for unsuccessful behaviors. The second enhances exploration during reinforcement learning through ambiguity-driven trajectory selection, enabling the policy to learn from more informative driving scenarios.

Failure-Aware Teacher Feedback

The original ReCogDrive framework relies solely on scalar rewards, making it difficult for the policy to understand why a trajectory fails. We introduce a teacher model that analyzes unsuccessful behaviors and provides semantic feedback such as collision risk, lane deviation, or unsafe maneuvers. This feedback is then used to regenerate trajectories and guide policy optimization, allowing the agent to learn corrective behaviors rather than only receiving sparse reward signals.

Ambiguity-Driven Exploration

Diffusion-based planners often generate highly similar trajectories, resulting in weak policy gradients and limited exploration. To address this issue, we generate multiple candidate trajectories and prioritize ambiguous samples that contain richer learning signals. By focusing on informative scenarios where different behaviors lead to different outcomes, the reinforcement learning process receives stronger supervision and can discover more robust driving policies.

We evaluate the proposed improvements on top of the ReCogDrive baseline. The results demonstrate that both reinforcement learning and ambiguity-driven trajectory selection consistently improve driving performance, leading to higher PDMS scores across different model scales.
